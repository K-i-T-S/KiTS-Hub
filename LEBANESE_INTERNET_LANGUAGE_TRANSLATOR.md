# Lebanese Internet Language (Arabizi) Translator

## Introduction

Lebanese Internet Language, commonly known as "Arabizi" (Arabic + Inglizi/English), is a modern dialect used primarily in online communication, social media, and text messaging. It combines English letters, numbers, and some Arabic characters to represent Lebanese Arabic words and phrases. This system emerged due to the limitations of early technology and keyboards that didn't support Arabic script, and has evolved into a cultural phenomenon among younger generations in Lebanon and across the Arab world.

## The Arabizi Alphabet System

### Number-to-Letter Correspondences

#### 2 = أ and ء (Aalif & Hamza)
- Represents the glottal stop sound
- Used at the beginning of words or within words
- Examples:
  - 2na = أنا (ana) = I/me
  - 2m = أم (umm) = mother
  - 2b = أب (ab) = father
  - 2ah = آه (ah) = oh/ah

#### 3 = ع (Ayn)
- Represents the distinctive Arabic "ayn" sound (voiced pharyngeal fricative)
- No equivalent in English pronunciation
- Examples:
  - 3arabi = عربي (arabi) = Arab
  - 3alem = عالم (alem) = world
  - 3adi = عادي (adi) = normal/okay
  - 3ayni = عيني (ayni) = my eye
  - 3ala = على (ala) = on/upon
  - 3am = عم (am) = doing (present continuous)

#### 5 = خ (Khaa)
- Represents the "kh" sound like the "ch" in Scottish "loch"
- Examples:
  - 5ayr = خير (khayr) = good
  - 5alas = خلص (khalas) = enough/done
  - 5odra = خضرة (khodra) = vegetables
  - 5amsa = خمسة (khamsa) = five
  - 5araj = خراج (kharaj) = exit/tax

#### 6 = ط (Taa)
- Represents the emphatic "t" sound
- Examples:
  - 6areeq = طريق (tareeq) = road
  - 6alab = طلب (talab) = request
  - 6abek = طابق (tabaq) = floor/level
  - 6are2 = طارق (tareq) = knocking

#### 7 = ح (Haa)
- Represents the harsh "h" sound from the throat
- Examples:
  - 7abibi = حبيبي (habibi) = my love/dear
  - 7al = حال (hal) = situation
  - 7ayat = حياة (hayat) = life
  - 7elwe = حلوة (helwe) = beautiful/sweet
  - 7mar = حمر (hamar) = red

#### 8 = ق (Qaaf)
- Represents the "q" sound (uvular stop)
- In Lebanese dialect, often pronounced as a glottal stop
- Examples:
  - 8alb = قلب (qalb) = heart
  - 8ahwa = قهوة (qahwa) = coffee
  - 8am = قام (qam) = stood up
  - 8raya = قراية (qraya) = story
  - 8aws = قوس (qaws) = bow

#### 9 = ص (Saad)
- Represents the emphatic "s" sound
- Examples:
  - 9aba7 = صباح (sabah) = morning
  - 9ama3 = صمع (sama) = sky
  - 9a7 = صح (sah) = health/correct
  - 9gha = صغى (sagha) = listened

### Additional Number Representations

#### 4 = ش (Shin)
- Sometimes used for the "sh" sound
- Examples:
  - 4akl = شكل (shakl) = form/shape
  - 4ams = شمس (shams) = sun
  - 4ari = شاري (shari) = buyer

#### 9' = غ (Ghayn)
- Represents the "gh" sound (voiced uvular fricative)
- Examples:
  - 9'ali = غالي (ghali) = expensive
  - 9'orba = غرب (gharb) = west
  - 9'ayeb = غريب (ghareeb) = strange

## Common Lebanese Internet Slang and Phrases

### Greetings and Basic Expressions

#### Marhaba = مَرحباً (Hello)
- Variations: Mar7aba, Mar7abeh
- Used: Formal and informal greetings

#### Shou fi ma fi? = شو في ما في؟ (What's up?)
- Literal: "What's there and what's not?"
- Usage: Casual conversation opener
- Arabizi: shu fi ma fi?

#### Kifak? = كيفك؟ (How are you?)
- To male: Kifak?
- To female: Kifik?
- Plural: Kifkon?
- Arabizi: kifak? kifik? kifkon?

#### Mni7 = منيح (Good/Fine)
- Response to "How are you?"

### Pronouns in Lebanese Internet Language

#### Personal Pronouns
- Ana = أنا (I/me) → 2na
- Enta = أنتَ (you - male) → enta
- Enti = أنتِ (you - female) → enti
- Howwe = هوّ (he/him) → howwe, huwe
- Hiyye = هيّ (she/her) → hiyye, hiye
- Ne7na = نحنا (we) → ne7na, n7na
- Entu = أنتم (you - plural) → entu
- Homma = همّ (they) → homma

#### Possessive Pronouns
- -i = ي (my) → i
- -ak = ك (your - male) → ak
- -ik = كِ (your - female) → ik
- -o = ه (his) → o
- -a = ها (her) → a
- -na = نا (our) → na
- -kon = كم (your - plural) → kon
- -on = هُن (their) → on

### Common Verbs and Their Conjugations

#### To Be (Kan/ken)
- Ana kan/kent = أنا كنت (I was)
- Enta kent = كنتَ (you were - male)
- Enti kenti = كنتِ (you were - female)
- Howwe kan = كان (he was)

#### To Do (3am y3mel)
- Ana 3am 3mel = أنا عم عمل (I am doing)

- Enti 3am t3mli = أنتِ عم تعملي (you are doing - female)

- Hiyye 3am t3mel = هي عم تعمل (she is doing)

- Enti baddik = أنتِ بدّك (you want - female)
- Howwe baddo = هو بدّو (he wants)
- Hiyye badda = هي بدّا (she wants)

#### To Go (Re7/rou7)
- Ana ra7 = أنا راح (I went)
- Enta re7t = أنت راحت (you went - male)
- Enti re7t = أنتِ راحت (you went - female)
- Howwe re7 = هو راح (he went)
- Hiyye re7et = هي راحت (she went)

### Common Lebanese Proverbs in Arabizi

#### "El-kelb 3al 2arab, wel 2arab 3al kelb" = الكلب على العرب والعرب على الكلب
- Meaning: It's a dog-eat-dog world
- Literal: The dog on the Arab, and the Arab on the dog

#### "El-ghala 2amla 7alha" = الغلة عملت حالها
- Meaning: Things will work themselves out
- Literal: The milk made itself

#### "Men shghar 3al 2amar, 2amlo 2ammar" = من صغر على القمر عمله أقمر
- Meaning: From a young age, he was ambitious
- Literal: From small on the moon, he made it brighter

#### "El-yom 3alik, w ba3den 3aleik" = اليوم عليك وبعدين عليك
- Meaning: Today it's your turn, tomorrow it's mine
- Literal: Today on you, and then on you

### Regional Variations

#### Age Group Differences
- Younger generations (Gen Z) use more abbreviations

### Tips for Understanding Lebanese Internet Language

#### Context is Key
- Same numbers can represent different letters based on context
- Regional variations exist
- Personal style affects spelling choices

#### Common Patterns
- Numbers usually replace letters with no English equivalent
- Vowel sounds are often implied rather than written
- Short forms are common in texting
- Emojis often accompany Arabizi for clarity

#### Learning Strategy
- Start with common numbers (3, 7, 2)
- Learn basic greetings and questions
- Practice with native speakers
- Use context to understand unfamiliar terms
